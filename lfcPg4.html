Liverpool FC Hall Of Legends

Steven Gerrard

Steven Gerrard is one of Liverpool Football Club's most iconic players, having spent his entire professional career with the team from 1998 to 2015. The midfielder was known for his technical ability, leadership qualities, and passion for the club. During his time at Liverpool, Gerrard won numerous titles, including the UEFA Champions League, UEFA Cup, FA Cup, and the English Premier League. He also scored crucial goals in many of Liverpool's most memorable matches, becoming a hero to the club's fans. Gerrard was named Liverpool's captain in 2003, a role he held until his departure in 2015. He remains a legend at the club and is widely regarded as one of the greatest players in Liverpool's history.

  • Years at Club: 1998-2015
  • Apperances: 504
  • Goals: 186
  • Assists: 97
  • Trophies:
    • Champions League: 2005
    • UEFA Cup: 2001
    • FA Cup: 2001, 2006
    • League Cup: 2001, 2003, 2012
    • Community Shield: 2006

John Barnes

John Barnes is a retired Jamaican-born English footballer who played for Liverpool Football Club from 1987 to 1997. He was a versatile player who primarily played on the left-wing, but also operated as an attacking midfielder and left-back when needed. Barnes was known for his pace, dribbling ability, and powerful left foot, which helped him score many goals for Liverpool during his tenure. He won two English First Division titles, two FA Cups, and the League Cup during his time at Liverpool, and was a key player in the team's success in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Barnes was also a regular member of the England national team, earning 79 caps and scoring 11 goals. He is considered one of the greatest English footballers of all time. After retiring from playing, Barnes has also had a successful career as a television pundit and commentator.

  • Years at Club: 1987-1997
  • Apperances: 403
  • Goals: 108
  • Assists: 83
  • Trophies:
    • League Championship: 1988, 1990
    • FA Cup: 1989, 1992
    • League Cup: 1995
    • Charity Shield: 1988, 1989, 1990, 1996

Ian Rush

Ian Rush is a retired Welsh footballer who spent two separate spells at Liverpool Football Club from 1980 to 1987, and again from 1988 to 1996. He is considered one of the greatest goal-scorers in Liverpool's history, having scored a total of 346 goals in 660 appearances across all competitions. Rush won numerous titles during his time at Liverpool, including five English First Division titles, three FA Cups, and two European Cups. He was also awarded the PFA Young Player of the Year award in 1983 and the FWA Footballer of the Year award in 1984. Rush was known for his speed, clinical finishing, and tireless work rate, and was a key player in Liverpool's success in the 1980s. After retiring from playing, he has had a successful career as a football coach and has worked in various roles at Liverpool Football Club, including as an ambassador for the club.

  • Years at Club: 1980-1987, 1988-1996
  • Apperances: 660
  • Goals: 346
  • Assists: 63
  • Trophies:
    • League Championship: 1982, 1983, 1984, 1986, 1988, 1990
    • FA Cup: 1986, 1989
    • League Cup: 1981, 1982, 1983, 1984, 1995
    • Charity Shield: 1982, 1986, 1988, 1989, 1990, 1991, 1992, 1996

Kenny Dalglish

Kenny Dalglish is a retired Scottish footballer and former manager of Liverpool Football Club. He is considered one of Liverpool's greatest ever players, having won numerous titles during his playing career, including six English First Division titles, three European Cups, and two FA Cups. Dalglish was known for his technical ability, vision, and clinical finishing, and was the club's top scorer in six of his eight seasons at Liverpool. After retiring from playing, he became Liverpool's player-manager in 1985 and led the team to a league and cup double in his first season. Dalglish went on to win two more league titles and another FA Cup during his second spell as Liverpool manager from 2011 to 2012. He is highly regarded by Liverpool fans for his contributions to the club as both a player and a manager, and is considered a legend of the game.

  • Years at Club: 1977-1990
  • Apperances: 515
  • Goals: 172
  • Assists: 65
  • Trophies:
    • League Championship: 1979, 1980, 1982, 1983, 1984, 1986, 1988, 1990
    • FA Cup: 1986, 1989
    • League Cup: 1981, 1982, 1983, 1984
    • European Cup: 1978, 1981, 1984
    • Super Cup: 1978, 1984

Fernando Torres

Fernando Torres is a retired Spanish footballer who played for Liverpool Football Club from 2007 to 2011. He was one of the most prolific strikers in the world during his time at Liverpool and was known for his pace, technical ability, and clinical finishing. Torres played a key role in the team's success during the late 2000s. He helped Liverpool reach the UEFA Champions League semi-finals in 2008 and was instrumental in the team's run to the final in 2010. Torres also won the PFA Fans' Player of the Year award in 2009 and was included in the PFA Team of the Year in both 2008 and 2009. Despite leaving Liverpool to join Chelsea in 2011, Torres remains a popular figure among Liverpool fans and is regarded as one of the club's greatest ever strikers.

  • Years at Club: 2007-2011
  • Apperances: 244
  • Goals: 81
  • Assists: 20
  • Trophies:
    • UEFA Champions League: 2012
    • UEFA Europa League: 2010
    • FA Cup: 2012
    • Community Shield: 2009

Robbie Fowler

Robbie Fowler, also known as "God" by Liverpool fans, is a retired English footballer who played for Liverpool Football Club during two spells, from 1993 to 2001 and from 2006 to 2007. Fowler is regarded as one of the greatest goalscorers in Liverpool's history. He won numerous titles during his time at Liverpool, including two English League Cups, an FA Cup, and a UEFA Cup. Fowler was known for his poacher's instinct, technical ability, and his love for the club, having grown up as a Liverpool fan. He is the sixth-highest scorer in Liverpool's history and was inducted into the club's Hall of Fame in 2007. Fowler also played for the England national team, earning 26 caps and scoring 7 goals. After retiring from playing, he has worked as a football coach and pundit.

  • Years at Club: 1993-2001, 2006-2007
  • Apperances: 357
  • Goals: 183
  • Assists: 33
  • Trophies:
    • League Cup: 1995, 2001
    • FA Cup: 2001
    • UEFA Cup: 2001
    • UEFA Super Cup: 2001
